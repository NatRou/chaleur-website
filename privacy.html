Privacy is the first decision, not a policy added at the end.

chaleur is being designed to handle the most sensitive information — who a person cares for, and why they are vulnerable — without that information ever leaving the device it was entered on. The architecture treats a bespoke privacy mistake as fatal to the project, so the boundaries are built in rather than promised.

Keeping the data on the device answers where it lives, not whether the person agreed to it, and a data-protection reviewer will ask both. A person can set chaleur up for themselves and consent for themselves; where a carer sets it up for someone who can consent, the carer records that they have agreed; and where the person cannot consent or does not know a profile exists, the information is held only on the carer's device and never in a central register, with a full consent and ethics framework for any pilot designed alongside a clinical and ethics partner. The two paths are set out in full on the Origins page.

Where data lives

Sensitive data stays on the device.

Health details and contacts are held locally and are designed never to sit on a server.

What gets shared

Coordination shares almost nothing.

When co-carers coordinate, only a thin, non-personal signal passes between them, never a person's identity or condition.

How it is checked

Independent review before any real use.

Encryption, key management and data-protection design are slated for external expert review as release gates, not self-certified.

Independent review is part of the plan, not a promise.

That review is one of the first tasks a clinical or institutional partnership would take on.
